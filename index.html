Rejection Challenge 30 days in person one rep per day

Train your brain to handle rejection in 30 days.

Most people avoid "no" so hard they stop taking shots in career and life. This challenge turns rejection into a skill: one small in-person ask per day, logged in a simple tracker, so confidence grows through reps.

What counts as a rep

  • It must be in person (not online or phone).
  • A rep counts if you asked something specific and you felt slightly vulnerable.
  • A rep counts if they say no. You thank them and stop.
  • No ridiculous asks. Keep it human and safe.

Why this works

Fear of rejection is normal. Repeated, controlled exposure reduces the emotional sting over time. When you treat rejection as data, you take more shots and learn faster.
- Less anxiety about asking
- More shots on goal (career, relationships, sales)
- Faster learning from real feedback

Ask scripts

Coffee chat
"Hey, quick one. I'm working on being more proactive. Would you be open to a 10-minute coffee chat sometime this week? Totally okay if not."
Feedback
"I'm trying to improve fast. Could I get 30 seconds of feedback on what I could do better next time?"
After a no
"Totally get it, thanks anyway. If you're open to sharing: what's one thing I could improve for next time?"

Daily reflection

  • What did I fear would happen?
  • What actually happened?
  • What did I learn and what is my next rep?
The goal is not "get yes." The goal is "take reps without spiraling."
